## Realistic Strategy: India → Canada Clients

### Phase 1 — Preparation (Week 1–2)
- Set up profiles on all 3 platforms
- Use a professional headshot
- Write a bio: "Next.js & TypeScript developer specializing in fast, accessible web apps for Canadian businesses"
- Add your portfolio link (deployed on Vercel with a custom domain)
- Set timezone in bio: "Available during EST/PST business hours"
- Add 2–3 project case studies with screenshots and outcomes

### Phase 2 — Smart Prospecting (Week 3–6)
- **Contra:** Post 3 portfolio projects as "services" (e.g., "Modern Landing Page in Next.js — $800"). Engage with Canadian startup founders.
- **Jobbers.io:** Apply to 2–3 postings daily. DM clients directly with a Loom video (90 seconds) showing their current site + what you'd improve.
- **Upwork:** Buy extra Connects. Apply to jobs posted in the last 1–4 hours only. Filter for Canada/US, $1K+ budget, <5 proposals. First 2 contracts: accept $15–$20/hr to get 5-star reviews fast.

---

## Strategies to Reduce Competition Instantly

1. **Time-zone targeting:** Apply at 7–9 AM EST (5:30–7:30 PM IST) to be among the first.
2. **Loom video proposals:** Record a 60–90 second screen recording showing their current website/problem and your solution. Your face in the corner builds trust.
3. **Niche down aggressively:** Target a specific niche (e.g., "I build fast Next.js websites for Canadian SaaS startups").
4. **"Free audit" cold outreach on LinkedIn:** Find Canadian small businesses with slow/ugly websites. Send a Loom video audit.
5. **Use Canadian English:** Small detail, big trust signal.
